Process & Approach

From Workshop to the Himalayan Foothills

[ Describe the logistical challenge of transporting bamboo structures to Ziro, Arunachal Pradesh — a remote location accessible via limited road connections. The need for components that are flat-packable, lightweight, and designed for rapid assembly by a small crew on-site. ]

[ Design considerations for an outdoor festival: structural stability for large open spans that can handle wind loads across the valley, crowd safety requirements for the stage and pavilions, and weather resilience during the festival season. ]

[ The aesthetic intention: bamboo as a material that is indigenous to Arunachal Pradesh and resonates deeply with the festival’s ethos of celebrating northeastern culture and the natural environment. The visual impact of large bamboo structures against the dramatic Ziro Valley landscape. ]

  • Flat-pack component system for remote transport to Arunachal Pradesh
  • Rapid on-site assembly within tight festival setup timelines
  • Wind-load engineered open-span stage and canopy structures
  • Culturally resonant material choice — bamboo native to the region
  • Reusable or responsibly dismantled post-festival structures
